My name is Emily Giel and I’m writing to apply for Squarespace’s Product Marketing Coordinator role. Some of my students in Vietnam

I’m particularly passionate about Squarespace’s mission of empowerment in the digital world. After spending the past year as a Fulbright scholar in Vietnam, a country where technology and the web are causing explosive economic and cultural growth, I saw the impact that access to digital resources can have. I spent much of my spare time teaching my students about digital marketing techniques. I taught them to sell their art online, to make websites for their clubs, and to fundraise on social media. I became inspired to dedicate my career to making great technology accessible to those who need it most.


The very youthful Dropspot team and a screenshot from the Dropspot app

I have been drawn to product marketing since my freshman year of college, when I joined a team of novice software engineers to lead marketing on a startup called Dropspot. I learned the basics of marketing on the fly and created a full marketing plan along with brand and acquisition strategies, and I pitched Dropspot at a startup competition to win a $50,000 investment. I began to understand that product marketing is inextricably tied to user experience, and thinking from the user’s perspective helped me create interesting new strategies that uniquely fit the product.

Later on, I furthered my skills in product marketing as the marketing analytics intern at HBO, where I conducted in-depth marketing analysis on HBO’s streaming products, HBO Go and HBO Now. I proved myself to be a creative self-starter when I noticed trends in user data and turned those observations into an entire report on product subscriber churn. I found patterns in lapsed subscriptions and identified user types based on their risk of churn. I then formulated data-driven marketing recommendations that would elongate user subscriptions and optimize retention. Eventually, I presented these findings to HBO upper management and began to implement new product marketing strategies. At Squarespace, I would be able to apply my skills for in-depth data analytics, attention to detail, and creative problem solving in order to optimize Squarespace’s product marketing strategy.

Squarespace is a dream company for me because I see Squarespace as the perfect intersection between excellent technology, compelling marketing, and meaningful experiences for users. I’m a passionate hard worker who is excited to learn, and I would be a valuable asset on Squarespace’s product marketing team to further Squarespace’s mission of digital empowerment.

I look forward to hearing from you. Please look through my site to learn more about me and don't hesitate to reach out with questions.
