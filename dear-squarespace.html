The very youthful Dropspot team and a screenshot from the Dropspot app

I have been drawn to digital marketing since my freshman year of college, when I joined a team of novice software engineers to lead marketing on a startup called Dropspot. I learned the basics of marketing on the fly and created a full marketing plan along with brand and acquisition strategies for a growing new startup. My work on Dropspot helped us secure a $50,000 investment when I pitched at the Igniter startup challenge, and I learned to be impact-driven and have total ownership over my work.

Later on, I furthered my skills in digital marketing as the marketing analytics intern at HBO, where I conducted in-depth marketing analysis on HBO’s streaming products, HBO Go and HBO Now. I proved myself to be a creative self-starter when I noticed trends in user data and turned those observations into an entire report on subscriber churn. I found patterns in lapsed subscriptions and identified user types based on their risk of churn. I then formulated data-driven marketing recommendations that would elongate user subscriptions and optimize retention. Eventually, I presented these findings to HBO upper management and began to implement new digital marketing strategies.

I learned about paid search engine marketing as the assistant media analyst at Tailwind, a lead generation agency. While there, I wrote thousands of keywords, A/B tested ads, analyzed lead generation data, and monitored campaigns. I had the chance to put my strategic skills to work when I led a project for a nonprofit client, using research on donor motivations to create an entirely new landing page and brand new SEM campaigns to drive seasonal donations. At Squarespace, I would be able to apply my skills for in-depth data analytics, attention to detail, and creative problem solving in order to optimize Squarespace’s SEM strategy.

Squarespace is a dream company for me because I see Squarespace as the perfect intersection between excellent technology, compelling marketing, and meaningful experiences for users. I’m a passionate hard worker who is excited to learn, and I would be a valuable asset on Squarespace’s marketing team to further Squarespace’s mission of digital empowerment.
